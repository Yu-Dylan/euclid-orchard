Mission Statement

Euclid's Orchard serves to provide high quality handouts to teach those who are interested in a variety of subjects.

About Us

We are a group of students who enjoy learning and wanted to share that with others. Our name is Euclid's Orchard, which is an array of one-dimensional "trees" of unit height planted at the lattice points in one quadrant of a square lattice. In our orchard we serve:
  • Douady Rabbit
  • Ham Sandwich (Theorem)
  • Chicken Mcnugget (Theorem)
  • Minkowski Sausage
If it isn't obvious, these are all parts of math! I recommend you search these up - some of these are advanced, but its always fun to learn about weirdly named theorems. There are a few very instrumental people to the succsss of this group. Although originally founded by me ("me" will always refer to the owner of this website, Dylan) and naman12 (who was extremely helpful - the inspiration for this whole webpage was naman12), we have branched out to other writers, who will be writing soon! General Handouts

These handouts cover multiple ranges (i.e. the ones detailed below).
  1. One Page Summaries V1.0 (One Page Summaries)
    2. Written by naman12 and me. This was a project to condense one topic into one page by picking what we thought was important. This is by no means a comprehensive guide - it is one page after all! I recommend reading this for review, or even just for fun. We hope you enjoy! And feel free to suggest other ideas!     AIME-USA(J)MO Handouts

    These handouts range from intermediate AIME to introductory USA(J)MO.
    1. Polynomials in the AIME (
      PDF Version
      ) (
      Table of Contents
      )
      2. This was written by naman12 and me. A complete guide on how to use polynomials on the AIME. Includes (almost) every polynomial problem on the AIME and also problems from other sources (such as RMO and HMMT).
    2. Proofs in Pre-USA(J)MO (
      PDF Version
      ) (
      Table of Contents
      )
      3. Written by Jai Sharma. Introduces proofs very nicely - so big thanks to him!
    3. Trigonometry in the AIME and USA(J)MO (
      PDF Version
      ) (
      Table of Contents
      )
      4. This was written by naman12 and me. A complete guide on how to use trigonometry on the AIME and USA(J)MO. Includes (almost) every trigonometry problem on the AIME, with worked out problems as well as more than one hundred hints to selected problems. There quite a few olympiad problems sprinkled in, too.
